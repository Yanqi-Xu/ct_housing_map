Connecticut Affordable Housing Rate by Town, 2002 - 2018
Fewer than one in five Connecticut towns has more than 10% of housing units available at an affordable price. For a unit to be considered affordable, it should cost no more than 30% for families earning 80% of average median household income and below.
In Westport, for instance, where the median income quadruples the national level, the rate is only 3.37%. Families making slightly less than the median income will have to try their luck with a limited supply of affordable housing, bite the bullet and pay for high rents or mortgages, or relocate.
